What Is an NDA?

A Non-Disclosure Agreement (also called a confidentiality agreement) is a legal contract that restricts what information you can share. It creates a legally binding obligation to keep certain information secret.

The 7 Key Sections of Every NDA

1. Definition of Confidential Information

This is the most important section. It defines what you can't share. Look for overly broad definitions like "all information" or "any materials." A good NDA specifically describes what's confidential.

4. Exclusions

Standard exclusions should include: information already public, information you already knew, information received from third parties, and information independently developed.

5. Permitted Disclosures

Can you share with your lawyer? Your accountant? Employees? Make sure you have reasonable rights to share with people who need to know.

6. Return/Destruction of Information

What happens when the NDA ends? You may need to return or destroy all confidential materials.

7. Remedies

What happens if you breach? Watch for "injunctive relief" (they can get a court order immediately) and "liquidated damages" (predetermined penalty amounts).

How to Negotiate an NDA

Yes, you can negotiate NDAs. Here's what to ask for:

  • Make it mutual (both parties bound)
  • Narrow the definition of confidential information
  • Add standard exclusions if missing
  • Set a reasonable time limit (2-3 years)
  • Remove any non-disparagement or non-compete language
  • Ensure you can share with your advisors
