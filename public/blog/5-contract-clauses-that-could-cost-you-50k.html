5 Contract Clauses That Could Cost You $50,000

1. Unlimited Liability

This clause means there's no cap on how much you could owe if something goes wrong. If your client's business loses revenue and blames your work, you could be liable for their entire loss.

"Contractor shall be liable for all damages, losses, and expenses arising from the performance of services, without limitation."
‚úÖ Fix: Negotiate a liability cap ‚Äî typically 1-2x the contract value. "Total liability shall not exceed the fees paid under this agreement."

2. Overbroad Non-Compete

üí∞ Potential cost: $50,000+ in lost income

Some non-competes prevent you from working in your entire industry for 1-2 years after the contract ends. As a freelancer, this could mean zero income for months.

"Contractor agrees not to provide similar services to any competing business within the United States for a period of 24 months following termination."
‚úÖ Fix: Narrow the scope. Limit to direct competitors, specific geographic area, and 3-6 months max.

3. Blanket IP Assignment

üí∞ Potential cost: Your entire portfolio + future work

This clause transfers ownership of everything you create ‚Äî not just project deliverables, but potentially your side projects, tools, and pre-existing work.

"All work product, inventions, and intellectual property created by Contractor during the term of this agreement shall be the exclusive property of Company."
‚úÖ Fix: Limit IP assignment to deliverables specifically created for this project. Exclude pre-existing IP and work outside scope.

4. Unilateral Termination Without Pay

üí∞ Potential cost: Months of unpaid work

Some contracts let the client terminate at any time without paying for work already completed. You could do 3 months of work and walk away with nothing.

"Company may terminate this agreement at any time for any reason. Upon termination, no further payments shall be due."
‚úÖ Fix: Add a "kill fee" clause: payment for all work completed to date, plus 30 days notice required.

5. Personal Guarantee in Business Contracts

üí∞ Potential cost: Your house, car, savings

If you signed a contract through your LLC but it includes a personal guarantee, your personal assets are at risk. The entire point of an LLC is gone.

"Principal personally guarantees all obligations of the business entity under this agreement."
‚úÖ Fix: Strike the personal guarantee entirely, or limit it to a specific dollar amount far below your personal assets.
